**Alistair**

Jachin stood on the podium, a sea of applause washing over him. Thousands of beings, each a unique voice in the symphony of approval that never lost its thrill. It was an intoxicating mix of adulation and power, and it was all for him. He bowed deeply, acknowledging the crowd, then stepped off the podium, descending into the labyrinthine underbelly of the court.

The familiar chatter of his advisors and bodyguards buzzed around him like a swarm of bees, their discussions of security and politics merely background noise. He'd grown weary of these conversations centuries ago. Now, he simply let them wash over him, their voices blending with the distant echoes of the crowd above.

Jachin's rise to prominence had been anything but ordinary. It was the schism that had paved his path, a fierce conflict that had torn the courts apart. He had been an advisor to the Darkheart family then, a position that felt far too small for his ambitions. As he navigated the shadowy corridors of the court now, at the helm of it all, he knew he'd been right.

His strategic move during the schism had been ruthless, yet effective. He'd given the opposition access to the Darkheart estate, and they'd taken care of the rest. The night raid, the brutal massacres, and the absolute silence that followed - it was a heinous act that had shocked the courts. And Jachin had been there, conveniently armed with proof of the opposition's guilt.

  
Now, with the weight of almost a millennium on his shoulders, he stood at the precipice of his life. He had navigated through hardships, had basked in greatness, and now, as the sun set on his extraordinary life, one truth reigned supreme over all others. He wasn't merely a survivor, an advisor, or a leader. Beyond titles and power, beyond the courts and the applause, he was the embodiment of an era, the personification of the fey. Jachin was not just the sovereign of the courts; he _was_ the Fey.
